# Hip Exercises

Use this plan for front/outside hip tightness or mild hip flexor irritation when there is no severe pain, trauma, numbness, fever, or inability to bear weight. Keep all work in a comfortable range. Pain during exercises should stay at `0-3/10` and should not be worse the next morning.

This is not a diagnosis. If there is deep groin pain, catching, locking, a hard block in range, limping, or no improvement after 2-4 weeks, get assessed by a physiotherapist or sports medicine clinician.

## Rules

- Do not force knee-to-chest, deep squats, deep lunges, or aggressive external rotation.
- Avoid jumping, sprinting, high step-ups, and explosive work until step-ups are pain-free and symmetrical.
- Add range first, then load, then speed.
- If symptoms flare the next day, reduce range or volume by half.

## Starter Routine

Do this routine `4-6 days/week` for the first week.

### 1. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

Sets: `2-3`

Time: `30 seconds each side`

How:

- Start in a half-kneeling position.
- Slightly tuck the pelvis under.
- Squeeze the glute on the kneeling side.
- Keep the ribs down and avoid arching the low back.

Stop if it causes a sharp front-hip pinch.

### 2. Quadruped Rock-Back

Sets: `2`

Reps: `10-15`

How:

- Start on hands and knees.
- Rock the hips backward slowly.
- Stop before any front-hip pinch.
- Try a slightly wider knee position if that feels better.

### 3. Glute Bridge

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `10-15`

How:

- Lie on your back with knees bent.
- Keep ribs down.
- Lift by squeezing the glutes, not by arching the low back.
- Pause briefly at the top.

### 4. Side-Lying Hip Abduction

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `10-15 each side`

How:

- Lie on your side.
- Keep the top leg straight and slightly behind the body.
- Point toes slightly down.
- Lift slowly without rolling the pelvis backward.

### 5. Dead Bug

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `6-10 each side`

How:

- Lie on your back with arms up and hips/knees bent.
- Keep the low back controlled.
- Move slowly.
- Only lower the leg as far as you can control without arching.

### 6. Low Step-Up

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `6-10 each side`

How:

- Use a low step.
- Step up slowly and lower slowly.
- Keep the knee tracking over the foot.
- Keep the pelvis level.

Start with a lower height if the left side hurts or feels weak.

## Optional Mobility

Add these after the starter routine feels comfortable.

### 90/90 Hip Switches

Time: `1-2 minutes`

Move side to side slowly. Use the hands behind the body for support. Keep the range small if the front of the hip pinches.

### Hip CARs

Sets: `1-2`

Reps: `3-5 each direction`

Do slow hip circles while standing or on all fours. Keep the pelvis controlled and avoid forcing the end range.

### Figure-4 Glute Stretch

Sets: `2`

Time: `30 seconds each side`

Lie on your back, place one ankle over the opposite knee, and gently pull in. Stop if it creates front-hip pinching.

## Strength Progression

Add these when the starter routine does not worsen symptoms.

### Clamshells

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `12-20 each side`

Keep the pelvis still and rotate the top knee open without rolling backward.

### Banded Lateral Walks

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `8-12 steps each direction`

Use small steps. Keep hips level and knees from collapsing inward.

### Split Squat

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `6-10 each side`

Use shallow range first. Stop if the front of the hip pinches.

### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `6-10 each side`

Start with bodyweight. Hinge at the hip and keep control through the foot, knee, and pelvis.

## Return to Jumping

Only start this when low step-ups are pain-free and left/right control feels similar.

### Small Jumps

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `5`

Use low effort. Land quietly. Stop if pain increases during the session or the next morning.

### Controlled Bounds

Sets: `2-3`

Reps: `5 each side`

Keep the distance short. Focus on control before power.

## Weekly Progression

Week 1:

- Starter routine only.
- No jumping or high step-ups.

Week 2:

- Continue starter routine.
- Add optional mobility and clamshells or lateral walks if symptoms are stable.

Week 3:

- Add shallow split squats or single-leg Romanian deadlifts.
- Start small jumps only if step-ups are pain-free.

Week 4:

- Gradually increase step height, squat depth, and jumping intensity.
- Keep the next-morning rule: no meaningful symptom increase the day after.
